## What PixSort Is
PixSort is an iPhone app that uses AI to automatically organize your camera roll into smart albums. No cloud sharing, no family albums — just your phone, your photos and videos, sorted.

**MVP Features:**
- Auto-sort camera roll into smart albums
- Screenshot / junk separation
- Duplicate detection and cleanup
- Content-based search (find photos by what's in them)
- Video clip organization (for creators)
- Custom album creation (user defines category, AI fills it)
- Push albums to camera roll (for sharing with editors or importing into CapCut/InShot)

**Co-founders:** Tiffany Parra (GTM, ICP research, interviews) + Spencer (product, engineering)
**Status:** TestFlight beta, App Store submission in progress
**Pricing:** Free tier → $9.99/month or ~$99/year. $99 lifetime founder's deal planned.

## ICP — Who We're Targeting

### Primary ICP (most validated)
**iPhone-first content creators who monetize and work with editors**
- Films multiple types of content on iPhone (B-roll, talking head, lifestyle)
- Has or wants to hire an editor — needs to share organized content
- Reuses B-roll across projects and clients
- Spends 5-10 minutes per editing session searching for clips
- Business-first mindset — understands time = money
- Examples: UGC creators, lifestyle creators, day-in-the-life creators

**Filter question:** "Do you shoot multiple types of content on your phone and need to find specific clips later?"

### Who is NOT ICP
- Event-based creators (DJs, event photographers) — recency-first workflow
- Social media managers who don't shoot their own content
- Serial deleters with disciplined habits
- Casual users — feel pain but won't pay

### Secondary ICPs (for Facebook ads testing)
1. **Parents with young kids** — emotional hook, huge Facebook audience
2. **Content creators with editors** — business ROI, most validated
3. **Family Archivist** — 30-55, managing decades of photos, highest WTP ($19.99/mo)
4. **Beauty/service providers** — nail techs, hairstylists, estheticians — shoot iPhone portfolio constantly, Facebook job title targeting is strong

## Customer Interview Findings

### Validated Pain Points (from 26 Reddit threads + 10+ interviews)
1. Overwhelming volume — 10K to 600K+ photos, years of backlog
2. Manual sorting is brutal — people literally "go mad"
3. Existing tools fail at scale — Google Photos, Apple Photos break at 500+
4. "The human is the index" — folder structures only work if you remember when/where
5. Screenshots and junk mixed with real photos — universally hated
6. No content-based search — people want "girl in red jacket" not date/location
7. Duplicates everywhere
8. Cross-platform chaos

## Landing Page Strategy

### Goal
Test 4 ICP angles via Facebook ads → collect phone numbers as proxy for download intent → SMS blast when App Store goes live.

### CTA approach
- Button: "Download Now — It's Free"
- Field: Phone number (not email — 98% SMS open rate vs 30% email)
- Post-submit: "You're in. We'll text you the moment PixSort is ready to download."
- Microcopy: "iOS only · Free to download · No spam"
