UGC Creator · $100K+ brand deals · App/software ads · Dad

Mom Test UGC Ads Dad Angle

The Rules

Don't mention PixSort until he independently describes the pain.
No hypotheticals. Only ask about things he's already done.
Don't fill silences. Wait — the real answer comes after the first one.
If it's going well, don't rush to the demo. Keep digging.

Open — His World First

Opening
"Walk me through what your actual filming week looks like — not the polished version, the real one."

Just listen. Don't steer yet. Note where friction comes up naturally.

Camera Roll Thread

When he mentions filming or sending content
"What happens to the footage after you film it? Where does it go?"

Then: "Has that ever caused a problem for you?"

If he says yes
"What did that actually look like? Walk me through that specific time."

Past behavior only. Don't suggest solutions.

If he says no
"How do you keep track of what you've filmed vs. what you've sent?"

Still useful — understand his system even if there's no pain.

App Ad Thread — Let Him Teach You

His expertise
"You've made UGC for apps specifically — what do you actually do differently when the thing you're selling isn't physical?"
What brands get wrong
"What's the single thing most app brands get wrong when they brief UGC creators?"
What makes it work
"When you watch an app ad that actually works — what's it doing in the first 3 seconds?"
His process
"When you get a brief for an app, what's your process from brief to final video?"

Dad Angle — Float Gently

Only if the conversation is warm and open
"You mentioned you're a dad — does that change how much content you have on your phone? Between work stuff and personal stuff?"

If he lights up: "How do you deal with that? Do you keep it separate or all in one place?"

Don't Do This

Don't ask "would you use something like this?" — people say yes to be polite.
Don't show the app until he's described the pain himself.
Don't explain PixSort as a solution before he's told you the problem.
Don't rush past a good answer — one deep thread beats five shallow ones.
